Happy Trotters

Volunteer

Located in North Salem, NY we are able to service children in the Westchester area and Western Connecticut. Hippotherapy allows therapists - physical, occupational and speech therapists to utilize the horses natural movement and disposition. Hippotherapy is a physical, occupational, and speech-language therapy treatment that utilizes equine movement as part of an integrated intervention program to achieve functional goals. Specially trained therapy professionals evaluate each potential rider on an individual basis to determine the appropriateness of including hippotherapy as a treatment strategy. The therapist then draws up a treatment plan deciding which horse, equipment and activities will promote the riders confidence and overall functional abilities. Therapists with this training know how to manipulate various aspects of the horse's movement that will translate to the rider reaching there goals.

New York Blood Center

Volunteer

Our mission is to serve our community & provide the highest quality in blood & stem sell products, donor and patient medical services, and innovative research. New York Blood Center collects, processes and distributes blood products from blood donors for transfusion to patients in area hospitals. Volunteers are an integral part of the donation process. They welcome donors, help with registration, serve refreshments and assist our staff to ensure that blood donors have a pleasant donation experience. Volunteer opportunities exist for morning, afternoons, evenings and weekends throughout the 5 Boroughs of New York City, the Hudson Valley area, New Jersey and Long Island. Our volunteers are asked to help at two or more blood drives each month. A blood drive is either 4 1/2 or 6 hours long. They are given a schedule to choose the drives that are most convenient to their lifestyle. All volunteers are required to attend a training class before they begin. We also utilize clerical volunteers in our offices.
